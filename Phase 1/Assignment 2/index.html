Quick facts about giraffes

  • Giraffes are the tallest mammals on Earth. Their legs alone are taller than many humans—about 6 feet.
  • They can run as fast as 35 miles an hour over short distances, or cruise at 10 mph over longer distances.
  • A giraffe’s spots are much like human fingerprints. No two individual giraffes have exactly the same pattern.

Quick facts about lions

  • A lion’s roar can be heard up to eight kilometres away.
  • A group, or pride, can be up to 30 lions, depending on how much food and water is available.
  • Female lions are the main hunters. While they’re out looking for food, the males guard the pride’s territory and their young.

Quick facts about elephants
  • An elephant's trunk weighs 400 pounds, but it can pick up things as small as a single grain of rice.
  • Elephants can recognize themselves in the mirror. They join humans, apes, and dolphins as the only animals with self awareness.
  • Elephants are born blind.
